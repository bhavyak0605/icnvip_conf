About Conference

Theme: Advancing Innovation in Nano-scale Semiconductor Devices, low power VLSI systems, and intelligent vision algorithms.

The International Conference on Nanoelectronics, VLSI, and Image Processing (ICNVIP-2027) aims to provide a premier global platform for researchers, academicians, industry professionals, and innovators to present and exchange groundbreaking ideas, original research, and technological advancements across interdisciplinary domains of modern electronics and intelligent computing.

The scope of the conference encompasses a broad range of cutting-edge topics organized across three core technical areas. In the domain of Nanotechnology and Nanoelectronics, the conference covers nanomaterials for electronic applications, advanced fabrication and characterization techniques, quantum technologies, optoelectronics, and wearable and flexible electronic devices. In the area of VLSI Design and Embedded Systems, it addresses low-power and high-performance circuit design, FPGA and SoC technologies, AI hardware accelerators, and neuromorphic computing architectures. In the field of Signal Processing and Image Processing, the conference explores computer vision, machine learning in imaging systems, biomedical imaging, and intelligent signal analysis.

By bringing together diverse perspectives from academia and industry, the conference seeks to foster meaningful collaboration, inspire high-quality research contributions, and accelerate innovation in next-generation electronic and computational technologies. It serves as a dedicated forum for addressing current challenges and unlocking future opportunities at the intersection of nanoelectronics, intelligent systems, and advanced imaging technologies.

About BVDUCOE

Bharati Vidyapeeth (Deemed to be University)

Bharati Vidyapeeth, the parent body of Bharati Vidyapeeth (Deemed to be University), was established in 1964 by the eminent educationist and visionary Dr. Patangrao Kadam. Bharati Vidyapeeth (Deemed to be University) (BV(DU)) is one of the topranking universities in India, committed to providing the best academic experience. It was established in 1996 in Pune with the aim of providing enhanced learning opportunities and bringing about the intellectual awakening of people through the spread of education that would have a positive impact on the world. Today, it has 8 campuses spread over different locations in India. BV(DU) has been consistently ranked among the Top 100 universities of India for nine consecutive years by NIRF, and achieved the 59th national rank in 2025 as per NIRF, Government of India. The University is also recognized with the prestigious A++ grade accreditation by NAAC and holds the Category-I University status by UGC, reaffirming its academic excellence and commitment to quality education.

BVDU College of Engineering, Pune

Established in 1983, BV(DU) COE offers 11 undergraduate, 9 postgraduate, and Ph.D. programmes across 8 disciplines. Programs are accredited by the National Board of Accreditation. The institute is ranked in the band of 151-200 by NIRF 2025. The institute is ranked among India’s top engineering institutes by DATAQUEST, Times of India, India Today, Outlook, and IIRF. Over 20,000 alumni of the institute from different disciplines are working worldwide. The institute has an excellent placement record. The institute’s vibrant research culture and advanced facilities make it a conducive platform for hosting an international conference of this scale.

Scope of Conference

Technical Topics

The conference welcomes original research contributions, review articles, and innovative case studies spanning a wide range of interdisciplinary topics organized across three core technical tracks:

Nanoelectronics and Nano Devices

This track focuses on the science and engineering of nanoscale materials and devices that are transforming modern electronics. The scope includes:

  1. Nanomaterials for Electronic Applications
  2. Nanomaterial Fabrication Techniques & Characterization
  3. Wearable and Flexible Nanoelectronic Devices
  4. Quantum & Emerging Technologies
  5. Optoelectronics & Photonic Devices

VLSI Design and Embedded Systems

This track addresses the design, optimization, and implementation of integrated circuits and embedded platforms for next-generation computing. The scope includes:

  1. Low Power and High Performance Circuits
  2. Analog, Digital & Mixed Signal Design
  3. FPGA, ASIC & System-on-Chip (SoC)

Signal Processing & Image Processing

This track explores advanced computational and AI-driven methods for signal analysis, visual perception, and intelligent imaging systems. The scope includes:

  1. Computer Vision & Pattern Recognition
  2. Machine Learning in Imaging Systems
  3. Biomedical Imaging & Applications
  4. AI Hardware & Neuromorphic Computing

The conference encourages submissions that bridge theoretical foundations with practical applications, promoting knowledge exchange and collaboration across academia and industry in the pursuit of next-generation electronic and intelligent computing technologies.
